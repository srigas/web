Hello, world!

My name is Spyros Rigas and I'm from Greece, currently living in Athens. In 2014 I started studying «Physics» in the Physics Department of the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens (NKUA), from which I earned my Bachelor's Degree in 2018. After my 9-month mandatory military service in Cyprus, I enrolled in the «Materials Physics» post-graduate studies programme at NKUA and earned my MSc diploma in 2021, after successfully defending my Master's Thesis titled «Solution of the tight-binding Bogolubov-de Gennes equations for topological superconductivity». While writting my Master's Thesis I came across several research papers using Machine Learning methods in order to study complex physical systems and the more I dove into the broader areas of Data Science and Machine Learning the more I felt attracted to them. This is why, while working as a post-MSc Researcher at NKUA, I also enrolled in the «Data Science & Machine Learning» inter-faculty post-graduate studies programme of the National Technical University of Athens (NTUA), with the plan to switch fields. Since I started my studies at NTUA I’ve undertaken a series of projects, some of which were part of the curriculum, while others were the product of personal curiosity. Most of them can be found in my GitHub page, however feel free to browse some of the most notable ones in my Projects page.

Music Genre Recognition and Sentiment Analysis

The dataset used for this project comprised of song samples corresponding to different music genres. The extracted features were mel-spectrograms and chromagrams, which were thereafter transformed into beat-synced versions thereof, in order to reduce their total sequence length. Covid-19 Vaccination Analysis

Description.

As part of my Master's Thesis I developed FORTRAN tight-binding code to perform simulations for one-dimensional topological superconductivity. The chosen platform was a superconducting surface consisting of 100 atomic layers and a chain of magnetic impurities in a spin-helix configuration embedded in or adsorbed on it. If you're interested, feel free to read this work here.

I am currently studying the effects of disorder on the aforementioned system, as well as general Green function properties for Superconducting Fermi Liquids with Spin-Orbit coupling.
